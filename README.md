# ☀️ Comparative & Temporal Analysis of Renewable Electricity and CO₂ Emissions (2010 – 2022)
### 🎯 Objective
Study how renewable electricity adoption rates relate to CO₂ emissions trends across multiple countries (2010–2022).
### 🧠 Background & Motivation
With increasing focus on climate change mitigation, national energy transitions provide measurable indicators of progress.  
This project evaluates renewable electricity generation and CO₂ emission trajectories using statistical and visual analysis.
### 🧰 Tools & Technologies
Python | Pandas | Seaborn | Matplotlib | NumPy | Statistical Modelling
### 📈 Methodology
1. Collected multi-country datasets on renewables and CO₂ levels  
2. Cleaned and merged disparate sources into standardized time-series  
3. Conducted **year-over-year trend analysis** and multi-country comparisons  
4. Visualized correlations using dual-axis plots and heatmaps  
5. Presented findings via an academic report and live presentation (awarded *Merit*)
### 🔍 Results & Insights
- Identified inverse correlations between renewable energy share and CO₂ emissions  
- Showed lag effects indicating gradual environmental impact  
- Provided data-driven recommendations aligning with UN sustainability goals

- Enhanced ability to communicate technical insights to non-technical audiences
